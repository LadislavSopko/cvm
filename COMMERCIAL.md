# Commercial Licensing

CVM is dual-licensed:

1. **Open source license** — [AGPL-3.0-or-later](./LICENSE) (default)
2. **Commercial license** — for use cases incompatible with AGPL-3.0

## Which license applies to you?

### You can use CVM under AGPL-3.0 for free if:

- You use CVM for personal projects, research, or learning
- You use CVM internally in your organization and do not distribute it or expose it as a network service to external users
- You distribute or operate a modified version of CVM **and** release your modifications (and any software combined with CVM into a larger work, including any service that uses CVM over a network) under AGPL-3.0
- You contribute back to the project

### You need a commercial license if:

- You want to integrate CVM into a **proprietary product** (closed-source, commercial software, SaaS, or any distribution that you do not wish to release under AGPL-3.0)
- You operate CVM as part of a **network-accessible service** for external users and cannot or do not want to release the surrounding service under AGPL-3.0
- You **bundle, redistribute, or embed** CVM in a commercial product, plugin, extension, or platform
- Your **legal or compliance policy prohibits AGPL-licensed code** in your stack
- You require **warranty, indemnification, or support guarantees** that AGPL-3.0 does not provide

## FAQ

**Q: I want to use CVM in a closed-source CLI tool I sell. Do I need a commercial license?**
A: Yes. Distributing CVM (or a derivative work that includes CVM) inside a closed-source product is not compatible with AGPL-3.0.

**Q: I run CVM on my laptop as part of my Claude Code workflow at work. Do I need a commercial license?**
A: No. Personal/internal use without redistribution or network service exposure is fine under AGPL-3.0.

**Q: My company runs CVM internally on a shared server that multiple developers connect to over the network. Do we need a commercial license?**
A: AGPL-3.0 section 13 requires that users interacting with CVM over a network receive the corresponding source code, including any modifications. If your internal users are inside the same legal entity and you have not modified CVM, this is typically considered internal use. If you have modified CVM or expose it to users outside your organization, you must comply with AGPL-3.0 or obtain a commercial license. When in doubt, contact us.

**Q: I want to fork CVM and build a competing open-source MCP execution engine. Can I?**
A: Yes. As long as your fork remains under AGPL-3.0 (or a compatible license) and you comply with its terms, you are free to fork.

**Q: I want to integrate CVM into a commercial AI agent platform. Do I need a commercial license?**
A: Yes.
